Biography

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, born on February 19, 1630, was the founder of the Maratha Empire and a renowned warrior and leader. He was born in Shivneri Fort, near Pune, to Shahaji Bhosale and Jijabai. Shivaji's mother, Jijabai, instilled in him a sense of justice and leadership from a young age. He is remembered for his military prowess, innovative tactics, and establishment of a strong administrative system, all contributing to the foundation of the Maratha Empire. Shivaji maharaj was raised in a turbulent period of conflict, which fostered his leadership skills.
He was mentored by his mother, Jijabai, and by Brahmin Dadaji Konda-dev, who trained him in military warfare and administration.
By the age of 16, he had already gathered a band of followers and began his campaigns to establish his power.

Why I Admire Him

  • Founded the Maratha Empire in the 17th century
  • Master of guerrilla warfare and strategic military tactics
  • Promoted religious tolerance and respected all faiths
  • Established an efficient civil administration system
  • Built a strong navy to secure the coastline
  • Crowned as Chhatrapati in 1674 at Raigad Fort
